Calvin, Arminius, Augustine, Pelagius, and the Synod of Dort

Pelagius Augustine Calvin Arminius Synod of Dort
410 - Pelagius: Commentary on Romans
"Whenever I do good, it is not God who works in me but I who do it myself."
Key Idea: Pelagius denied original sin and taught that human beings are capable of choosing good without divine grace.
Quiz: What was a key teaching of Pelagius regarding human nature?
That grace is irresistible
That we are incapable of good without God
That humans can choose good without divine help
✅ Correct answer: That humans can choose good without divine help.
418 - Augustine: Against Two Letters of the Pelagians
"It is not because men believe that they are elected, but in order that they may believe, they are elected."
Key Idea: Augustine argued that God's grace is necessary for even the first steps of faith, and that election is unconditional.
1559 - Calvin: Institutes Final Edition
"All are not created on equal terms, but some are preordained to eternal life, others to eternal damnation."
Key Idea: Calvin emphasized God's sovereign choice in election — not based on anything foreseen in humans.
1608 - Arminius: Declaration of Sentiments
"Predestination is an eternal and gracious decree of God in Christ, by which He determines to justify and adopt believers."
Key Idea: Arminius believed God's election is based on foreseen faith, preserving human responsibility.
Quiz: Who believed that election depends on God's foresight of faith?
Calvin
Synod of Dort
Arminius
✅ Correct answer: Arminius.
1619 - Synod of Dort
"Election is the unchangeable purpose of God whereby, before the foundation of the world, He has out of mere grace... chosen... unto salvation."
Key Idea: The Synod of Dort reaffirmed Calvinistic views against Arminianism, especially unconditional election.
Quiz: What did Augustine argue was necessary even for the beginning of faith?
Human free will
God's grace
Church membership
✅ Correct answer: God's grace.
Quiz: Which council reaffirmed the doctrine of unconditional election?
Council of Trent

✅ Correct answer: Synod of Dort.
